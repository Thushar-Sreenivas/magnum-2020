Major jobs event aims to lure London tech talent to Ireland

16 JAN 2018
An upcoming London event is aiming to persuade skilled tech professionals to relocate to Ireland.

Tech/Life Ireland | 404 London will take place at Skills Matter | CodeNode, 10 South Place, London EC2M 7EB from 10am to 5pm on Saturday, 10th February.

The event will showcase exciting tech opportunities in Ireland, with leading employers such as Workday, Hubspot, CarTrawler, Indeed Prime, McAfee, and Bloomberg PolarLake among others hiring on the day. Hundreds of job opprtuntities will be available for experienced professionals in software, data, security, fintech, devops and other areas.

Announcing details of Tech/Life Ireland | 404 London, Jackie Slattery, event co-founder, said: “We’re bringing all that’s great about the Irish tech scene to London for this exclusive event.

“The line-up will include trailblazing speakers, amazing installations and the opportunity to network with Ireland’s leading tech employers with top career opportunities. Just one of the keynote sessions to look forward to is John and Brenda Romero of Romero Games who moved their business and their life from Silicon Valley to the small city of Galway in Ireland. There will also be key players from the Irish tech community and meet-up groups, so attendees can get a sense of what’s happening on the ground in Ireland.” 

Tech Opportunities in Ireland

According to IDA Ireland, the government agency that encourages inward investment by foreign-owned companies, Ireland’s scaling tech sector currently employs over 30,000 people, equating to almost 10 per cent of the working-age population.

  • Recent data from LinkedIn shows the UK accounts for the largest net migration to Ireland: 21 per cent of all professional talent migrating to Ireland now comes from the UK.
  • 9 of the top 10 global software companies and 9 of the top 10 US technology companies operate in Ireland.
  • 10 of the top 10 ‘born on the internet’ companies operate there.
  • 50 per cent of Ireland’s population is under 35, making it the youngest country in the EU.

“Ireland’s thriving tech scene is well-documented, as is the need for a pipeline of skilled professionals to ensure continued growth within the sector,” said Jackie Slattery.  “For globally-minded professionals with the right tech skills, Ireland has so much to offer.  Both in terms of career development and quality of life, the opportunities here are amongst the best in the world.”

Tickets for Tech/Life Ireland |404 London are priced £10 and are available now at: www.404.ie.  The event is sponsored by Workday, CarTrawler, Hubspot and Indeed Prime, and is being run in partnership with Tech/Life Ireland, an Irish government initiative to promote tech career opportunities in Ireland.
